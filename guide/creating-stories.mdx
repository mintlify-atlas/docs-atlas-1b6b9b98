Casa de Historias makes it easy to preserve and share indigenous legends. The story creation form guides you through capturing all aspects of a legend.

## Starting a New Story

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the story list">
    Navigate to the main story list screen (home).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap the + button">
    Tap the floating action button (circular + button) in the bottom-right corner.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in the story details">
    Complete the story creation form (detailed below).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Story Information Form

The form is organized into two main sections:

### Datos Generales (General Information)

This card contains identifying information about the story:

#### Cover Image (Optional)

- Tap the image placeholder to select a cover photo
- The image appears as a visual representation of the legend
- Box shows camera icon with "Añadir foto de portada" when empty

<Note>
  Cover images make your story more engaging and help it stand out in browse views.
</Note>

#### Required Fields

These fields are marked with an asterisk (*):

- **Título en Español** - The story title in Spanish
- **Nombre del Narrador** - Name of the person who shared this legend

#### Optional Fields

- **Título en Náhuatl** - Indigenous language title
- **Comunidad de origen** - Community where this legend originates

<Warning>
  Make sure all required fields are filled before submitting. The app will show error messages for any missing required information.
</Warning>

#### Map Location (Optional)

You can mark the geographic location associated with the legend:

1. A map view shows the Sierra de Zongolica region (centered at 18.6667°, -97.0000°)
2. Tap anywhere on the map to place a marker
3. The marker indicates where the story takes place or originates
4. Tap again to move the marker to a different location

The map uses MapLibre with OpenFreeMap tiles for an open-source mapping experience.

### La Historia (The Story Content)

This card contains the actual legend text:

#### Spanish Story (Required)

- **Historia en Español** - The complete story text in Spanish
- Multi-line text field (minimum 120dp height)
- This is the main content users will read

#### Náhuatl Story (Optional)

- **Historia en Náhuatl** - The story in the indigenous language
- Multi-line text field
- Preserves the original oral tradition

<Card title="Preserving Indigenous Language" icon="language">
  Including the Náhuatl version helps preserve the original language and allows speakers to experience the legend in its traditional form.
</Card>

## Publishing Your Story

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review your information">
    Scroll through the form to verify all details are correct.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap 'Publicar Historia'">
    The large button at the bottom publishes your story.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Wait for confirmation">
    A loading spinner appears while the story is being saved.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Return to story list">
    Upon success, you're automatically returned to the main list where your story now appears.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Form Validation

The app validates your input before publishing:

| Field | Validation |
|-------|------------|
| Título en Español | Cannot be blank |
| Nombre del Narrador | Cannot be blank |
| Historia en Español | Cannot be blank |
| All optional fields | No validation (can be empty) |

If validation fails:
- The field with the error is highlighted in red
- An error message appears below the field
- The publish button remains enabled so you can fix the errors and try again

## Loading States

While your story is being published:
- The "Publicar Historia" button shows a circular loading indicator
- The button is disabled to prevent duplicate submissions
- Wait for the process to complete

<Warning>
  Don't close the app or navigate away while the story is being published.
</Warning>

## Error Handling

If an error occurs during publishing:
- An error message appears above the publish button
- Read the message to understand what went wrong
- Correct any issues and tap "Publicar Historia" again

## What Happens After Publishing

Once published, your story:
- Appears in the main story list
- Shows up in the "Lo más nuevo" section of the Discover screen
- Becomes searchable by title, narrator, community, and tags
- Can be added to favorites by other users
- Appears on the map (if you included a location)

## Tips for Great Stories

<Card title="Best Practices" icon="lightbulb">
  - **Use descriptive titles** - Help people understand what the legend is about
  - **Include community information** - Connect the story to its geographic and cultural origin
  - **Add both language versions** - Preserve the indigenous language while making the story accessible
  - **Upload a cover image** - Visual elements make stories more engaging
  - **Mark the location** - Help people discover stories from specific regions
</Card>
