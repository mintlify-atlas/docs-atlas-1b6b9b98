The Náhuatl Dictionary helps you learn and understand indigenous words used in the legends. It features common words from the Sierra de Zongolica region.

## Accessing the Dictionary

You can open the dictionary from multiple places:

### From the Discover Screen

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Discover">
    Tap the "Explorar" tab in the bottom navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Scroll to the Learn section">
    Look for the "Aprender" section.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap the Dictionary card">
    Tap the "Diccionario Náhuatl" card with the gradient background.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Dictionary Card Appearance

- **Heading**: "Diccionario Náhuatl"
- **Description**: "Aprende palabras de la Sierra y su significado"
- **Icon**: Play arrow in a circular badge
- **Background**: Gradient from deep night to sacred water colors

## Dictionary Interface

### Search Bar

At the top of the screen:
- **Search icon**: Magnifying glass in sacred water color
- **Placeholder**: "Buscar palabra..."
- **Function**: Filters words as you type

The search works on:
- Náhuatl word spelling
- Spanish translation
- Case-insensitive matching

<Note>
  Type just a few letters to find words - you don't need to know the exact spelling.
</Note>

### Word Count

Below the search bar:
- Shows total number of words in the filtered results
- Format: "Palabras Comunes (10)" when showing all words
- Updates dynamically as you search

## Word Cards

### Card Layout

- Gray background box
- Format: "Ej: [example text]"

## Searching the Dictionary

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the search field">
    The keyboard appears and you can start typing.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Type your query">
    Enter part of a Náhuatl word or Spanish translation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View filtered results">
    The word list updates automatically to show only matching entries.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Clear the search">
    Delete your text to see all words again.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Search Examples

- Search "agua" → Shows "Atl" (water)
- Search "ton" → Shows "Tonatiuh" (sun)
- Search "gracias" → Shows "Tlazocamati" (thank you)

## Cultural Context

The dictionary emphasizes cultural significance:

### Nature Elements
- **Tonatiuh** (Sun) - "El dador de vida" (The life-giver)
- **Metztli** (Moon) - "La que brilla en la noche mística"
- **Tletl** (Fire) - "El abuelo fuego" (respecting fire as an elder)

### Food & Sustenance
- **Cintli** (Maíz) - "Nuestro sustento" (our sustenance)
- Shows the central importance of corn in indigenous culture

### Knowledge & Wisdom
- **Amoxmatqui** (Lector/Sabio) - "Quien conoce el libro"
- Honors the keepers of knowledge

<Warning>
  Náhuatl has regional variations. This dictionary focuses on words from the Sierra de Zongolica dialect.
</Warning>
