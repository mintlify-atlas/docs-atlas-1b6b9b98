The Map feature provides a geographic view of indigenous legends, showing where stories originate or take place. This helps you discover legends connected to specific locations.

## Accessing the Map

Navigate to the Map screen from the bottom navigation:

1. Look for the map icon in the bottom navigation bar
2. Tap "Mapa de Leyendas" or the map tab
3. The map loads centered on the Sierra de Zongolica region

## Map Interface

### Map Technology

Casa de Historias uses:
- **MapLibre** - Open-source mapping library
- **OpenFreeMap tiles** - Free and open map data
- **Liberty style** - Clear, readable map appearance

### Default View

The map opens centered on:
- **Coordinates**: 18.6667°N, 97.0000°W
- **Region**: Sierra de Zongolica, Veracruz
- **Zoom level**: 9.0 (regional view)

<Note>
  The Sierra de Zongolica is the heart of Náhuatl-speaking communities in Veracruz, where many of these legends originate.
</Note>

## Story Markers

Stories with location information appear as markers on the map.

### What Markers Show

Each marker represents:
- **Location**: The geographic coordinates where the story takes place or originates
- **Title**: Story name in Spanish
- **Snippet**: "Narrado por [narrator name]"

### Interacting with Markers

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap a marker">
    Tap any marker pin on the map.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View the story preview">
    A bottom sheet slides up showing:
    - Story title in Spanish
    - Story title in Náhuatl (if available)
    - Preview of the story content (first 3 lines)
    - Narrator information
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Read the full story">
    Tap "Leer leyenda completa" button to open the complete story detail.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Closing the Preview

To dismiss the bottom sheet:
- Swipe down on the drag handle
- Tap outside the sheet area
- The sheet closes and you return to the map view

## Navigating the Map

Standard map controls:
- **Pan**: Drag with one finger to move around
- **Zoom**: Pinch to zoom in/out
- **Rotate**: Two-finger rotate gesture (if enabled)

<Card title="Exploring Regions" icon="compass">
  Pan around the Sierra de Zongolica to discover stories from different communities and municipalities.
</Card>

## Empty States

If no stories have location data:

- Map icon emoji (🗺️)
- Heading: "Sin ubicaciones"
- Message: "Aún no hay historias con ubicación en el mapa"

<Note>
  Only stories created with a location marker appear on the map. Older stories without location data won't show markers.
</Note>

## How Location Data Works

When creating a story:
1. Authors can tap the map to mark a location
2. The coordinates (latitude and longitude) are saved with the story
3. The story appears as a marker on the Map screen
4. Users can tap the marker to discover the story

### Stories With Locations

The map filters the story collection to show only:
- Stories with both latitude and longitude data
- Valid coordinates within reasonable bounds
- Published stories (not drafts)

## Bottom Sheet Details

The story preview bottom sheet includes:

### Header
- **Title in Spanish**: Large, bold text
- **Title in Náhuatl**: Italic style in sacred water color (AguaSagrada)

### Content
- **Story preview**: First portion of the Spanish text
- **Text overflow**: Shows "..." if content is truncated
- **Max lines**: 3 lines of preview text

### Action Button
- **Button text**: "Leer leyenda completa"
- **Style**: Full-width, rounded corners, aqua color
- **Action**: Opens the full story detail screen

<Warning>
  The bottom sheet automatically closes when you navigate to the story detail. Use the back button to return to the map.
</Warning>

## Use Cases

<Card title="Why Use the Map?" icon="route">
  - **Geographic discovery** - Find legends from specific areas
  - **Cultural tourism** - Plan visits to locations mentioned in stories
  - **Community connection** - See how stories cluster in certain regions
  - **Education** - Understand the geographic context of legends
  - **Preservation** - Document where oral traditions originate
</Card>

## Tips for Map Exploration

- Zoom in to see markers more clearly in dense areas
- Explore different municipalities to discover regional variations
- Use the map alongside community filters on the Discover screen
- Encourage story creators to add locations when publishing

## Technical Details

### Map Data
- **Tile source**: https://tiles.openfreemap.org/styles/liberty
- **Map library**: MapLibre GL Native for Android
- **Coordinate system**: WGS84 (standard GPS coordinates)

### Performance
- Map tiles are cached for offline viewing (where supported)
- Markers are rendered efficiently even with many stories
- The map view pauses/resumes appropriately when navigating away

<Note>
  The map requires an internet connection to load new areas. Previously viewed areas may be available from cache.
</Note>
