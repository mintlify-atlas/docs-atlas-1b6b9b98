Casa de Historias provides multiple ways to discover and browse indigenous legends. Each screen offers a different perspective on the collection.

## Story List View

The main story list displays all published legends in a scrollable feed.

### What You'll See

Each story card shows:
- **Cover image** (if available) - Visual representation of the legend
- **Title in Spanish** (Título en Español) - Main title displayed prominently
- **Title in Náhuatl** (Título en Náhuatl) - Indigenous language title in italic style
- **Narrator name** - Who shared this story
- **Community** - Origin community of the legend
- **Audio indicator** - A play button icon if the story has audio narration

### Empty States

If there are no stories yet, you'll see:
- A scroll emoji (📜)
- Message: "No hay historias aún"
- Invitation to be the first to share a legend from your community

## Discover Screen

The Discover screen organizes stories into curated sections:

### Lo más nuevo (What's New)

Displays the 5 most recently published stories in a horizontal scrollable row. Each featured story card includes:
- Large cover image
- Story title in both Spanish and Náhuatl
- Narrator information

<Note>
  Swipe horizontally to browse through new stories quickly.
</Note>

### Con Audio Original (With Original Audio)

Shows stories that include audio recordings. These appear as compact cards with:
- Thumbnail image
- Play button indicator
- Story titles
- Narrator name

This section helps you find stories you can listen to in addition to reading.

### Aprender (Learn)

Features a prominent card linking to the Náhuatl Dictionary:
- "Diccionario Náhuatl" heading
- Description: "Aprende palabras de la Sierra y su significado"
- Tap to access the dictionary

### Por Comunidad (By Community)

Displays filter chips for each community that has contributed stories. Features:
- Community names as interactive chips
- Tap a chip to browse stories from that specific community
- Shows up to 10 unique communities

<Card title="No communities yet?" icon="info">
  If you see "No hay comunidades registradas," it means no stories have included community information yet.
</Card>

## Reading a Story

Tap any story card to view the full story detail screen.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap a story card">
    From any browse view, tap on a story card.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View the full story">
    The detail screen shows:
    - Full cover image (if available)
    - Complete story title in Spanish and Náhuatl
    - Narrator name and community
    - Full story text in Spanish
    - Story text in Náhuatl (if provided)
    - Tags for categorization
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use the toolbar">
    The top toolbar provides:
    - Back button (arrow) - Return to previous screen
    - Favorite button (heart) - Save to favorites
    - Edit button - Modify the story (if you have permission)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Story Content Layout

The story detail is organized in sections:

1. **Header Card** - Title, narrator, community, and tags with a subtle gradient background
2. **Story Content Card** - "La Historia" section with the full Spanish text
3. **Náhuatl Section** - If available, shows "En Náhuatl" heading with the indigenous language version

<Note>
  Stories with Náhuatl content display it in italic style to distinguish it from the Spanish text.
</Note>

## Search and Filter

Access the search feature from the story list:

1. Tap the search icon (🔍) in the top toolbar
2. Type your search query
3. Results update as you type
4. Search works across:
   - Story titles (Spanish and Náhuatl)
   - Narrator names
   - Communities
   - Tags

See the [Searching Stories](/guide/browsing-stories#search-and-filter) section for more details.

## Adding New Stories

Tap the floating action button (+) on the story list screen to create a new story. See [Creating Stories](/guide/creating-stories) for the complete guide.
